queer.code();

LGBTQ+ Coding Mentorship based in Seattle, WA

queer.code(); is a platform for individuals to come together to learn and create with code. Our mission is to empower people in the LGBTQ+ community with foundational programming skills necessary to make tangible whatever they envision.

Our Vision

At queer.code(); we are focused on dismantling stereotypes of what it means to be a person interested and passionate about technology. Systems of power, privilege, and oppression have traditionally created barriers for persons and groups with particular identities to thrive in the field of technology. To say that coding is not queer is a misconception, especially since some of the most notable pioneers in computation and programming are LGBTQ+. We want to make it known how important diversity is in the growing technological climate.

We foster purposeful inclusion, and strive to create an environment where all can feel valued and cared for. We value the diversity of racial and cultural identity, nationality, sexual orientation, gender identity, religion, age, and ability.

With ballooning rents displacing people all over the Seattle, we hope to help bridge the gap between marginalized communities and access to education in software literacy.

Mentorship

queer.code(); offers project based mentorships, stand alone workshops, and industry connections. Although we will cover a variety of languages and concepts, as a mentee of queer.code(); we hope to instill you with the following:

  • Understanding of basic coding concepts, syntax, and platforms to write code.
  • Knowledge of specialized topics based on project modules.
  • Network to programmers, designers, engineers, people in tech, and individuals new but excited to learn how to code.
  • Cool shit you programmed that you can show to your loved ones.
  • Personal and professional accounts on industry and tech platforms like Github and LinkedIn.
  • An online portfolio with content you created with us.
  • Click here for more information about our first mentorship project module, Flowers for Ingersoll and Freed!

queer.code(); will exist in a modular paradigm in which mentees will work over the course of a fixed amount of weeks to complete a project and master programming concepts. Mentees are asked to fully commit to attend each session within a module.
