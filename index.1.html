queer.code();

LGBTQ+ Coding Mentorship based in Seattle, WA

Community Statement

queer.code(); is a platform for LGBTQ+ individuals to come together to collaborate, learn, and create with code. We are focused on dismantling stereotypes of what it means to be a person in tech, and we are determined to take down barriers keeping people from learning to program. With ballooning rents displacing people all over the Seattle, we hope to help bridge the gap in access to education and job opportunities.

At queer.code(); we believe that representation and expression of one's true self is of upmost importance. In a field that is notorious for classism, sexism and tech bros, we want to make it known how important diversity is in a growing tech dependent climate. queer.code(); wants to give power back to the people so they can help shape the future.

queer.code(); is actively seeking co-collaborators, mentors and parties interested in leading workshops for individuals interested in coding but aren't part of the cohort/interested in different languages! Interested? Get involved!

Mentorship

queer.code(); offers project based mentorships, stand alone workshops, and industry connections. Although we will cover a variety of languages and concepts, as a mentee of queer.code(); we hope to instill you with the following:

  • Understanding of basic coding concepts, syntax, and platforms to write code.
  • Knowledge of specialized topics based on project modules.
  • Connection to programmers, designers, engineers, people in tech, and individuals new but excited to learn how to code.
  • Cool shit you programmed that you can show to your loved ones.
  • Personal and professional accounts on industry and tech platforms like Github and LinkedIn.
  • An online portfolio with content you created with us.
  • Click here for more information about our first mentorship module, queer.code(); vs. Arlene's Flowers!

queer.code(); will exist in a modular paradigm in which mentees will work over the course of a fixed amount of weeks to complete a project and master programming concepts. Mentees are asked to fully commit to attend each session.
