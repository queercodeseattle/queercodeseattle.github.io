queer.code();

LGBTQ+ Coding Mentorship based in Seattle, WA

Community Statement

queer.code(); is a platform for LGBTQ+ individuals to come together to collaborate, learn and create with code. We are focused on taking down barriers between creation and technology. With ballooning rents displacing people all over the city, we hope to help bridge the gap in relevant industry skills with peer mentorship with passionate programmers, workshops to teach individauls in the LGBTQ+ community with little to no coding background software literacy, and connect them with seasoned industry veterans.

Mentorship

As a mentee of queer.code(); we hope to instill you with the following:

  • Understanding of basic coding concepts, syntax, and paltforms to write code.
  • Connection to programmers, engineers, people in tech, industry connections.
  • Cool shit you programmed that you can show to your loved ones.
  • Accounts on industry/tech websites like Github, LinkedIn.
  • An online portfolio with content you created with us.

queer.code(); is looking for it's first cohort! In it's (beta) phase, queer.code(); will provide a weekly mentorship program working off open source material learning Object Oriented Programming concepts and building basic programs.

As queer.code(); is just getting started, it will operate on a 5:1, mentee to mentor ratio. If you are new or interested in coding, apply below to be part of the first cohort! Once the positions are filled, we will be set to meet on a weekly basis between Feburary and June!

queer.code(); is actively seeking co-collaborators, mentors and parties interested in leading workshops for individuals interested in coding but aren't part of the cohort/interested in different languages!

queer.code(); is set to meet every Saturday between 3:00 - 5:00PM at Kaladi Brother's Coffee in Capitol Hill, Seattle beginning Feburary 2019!
